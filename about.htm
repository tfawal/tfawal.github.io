Current Aspirations

I am currently attending UC Berkeley, and pursuing a backelor's degree in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science, with a minor in Music.

As far as current academic endeavors go, this summer I am taking on two of them. The first is that I am helping to edit and revise professor Ali Niknejad's EE105 Reader for the introductory microelectronics leader course at Cal. This is to both help reinforce my understanding of the material in preparation for EE140 (Analog Integrated Circuits) which I will be taking in the fall semester, and to help the students in Ali's class that will be taking EE105 in the fall as well.

The second endeavor is that I will be an academic intern for CS61C this summer. This was my favorite computer science course that I have taken at Cal. This will also help to reinforce my understanding of the material in preparation for taking EECS151 in the fall, where I will be making a RISC-V computer using an FPGA. I also hope to become part of the course staff for CS61C and EE105 in the fall semester.

I am beginning my search for an intership next summer of 2023, following my expected graduation date. I am someone that can adapt to and learn anything I set my mind to. I am a natural leader, and know how to get things done. I am open to any opportunity that sounds like interesting and inspiring work. That said, anything related to analog and digital integrated circuit design, electric motors, and sound engineering are at the top of my list. It is exciting to embark on this new journey and find the right company to make this happen with.
