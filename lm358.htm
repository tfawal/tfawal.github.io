I am also minoring in music, and during this time I made a guitar amplifier in an instrument design class using my new knowledge of circuits. I built everything by hand, sourcing the parts and using the makerspace in Jacobs Hall. I gained the experience of drawing out a circuit diagram based on reading the spec sheets of potential parts. Then I turned the design into a working amplifier by soldering the parts onto a PCB board, and installing it into a cabinet with a speaker. The amplifier produces noise-free clean and overdriven guitar tones with an LM386 op-amp. Six years ago I only knew what a good guitar amplifier sounded like, and now I can build them.
